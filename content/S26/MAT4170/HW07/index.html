Exercise 3 [6] You can follow along with this problem by watching the corresponding async lecture on YouTube. This is a long exercise, but it is only worth six points because you can do this problem by following along with the asynchronous lecture which will be available before Monday at 3PM.

Let $G$ be a group. Then we say that $a \in G$ is conjugate to $b \in G$ if there exists $g \in G$ such that $a = g b g^{-1}$. The relation is conjugate to is an equivalence relation, so it allows us to partition $G$ into equivalence classes (which we call conjugacy classes).

Intuitively, $a$ and $b$ are conjugate if performing $a$ from one perspective is the same as performing $b$ from another perspective. As an example, a horizontal flip and a vertical flip of a square are conjugate in $D_4$ because if you're standing $90^\circ$ to the right of me and I do a horizontal flip of the square, it will look like a vertical flip from your perspective—but a horizontal flip to me will never look like a $180^\circ$ rotation to you.

In the following exercises you will be looking at conjugacy classes of the symmetry group of the hexagon.

  1. Prove that $e$ is in its own conjugacy class.
  2. Find an example of $g \in D_6$ such that $r^2 = g r^4 g^{-1}$.
  3. Find an example of $g \in D_6$ such that $f = g r^2f g^{-1}$.
  4. Prove that if $\phi\colon D_6 \to H$ is a homomorphism, and $g_1$ is conjugate to $g_2$ in $D_6$, then $\phi(g_1)$ is conjugate to $\phi(g_2)$ in $H$.
  5. Prove that if $\phi\colon D_6 \to H$ is a homomorphism, $H$ is an abelian group, and $g_1$ is conjugate to $g_2$ in $D_6$, then $\phi(g_1) = \phi(g_2)$.
  6. Let $\Phi\colon D_6 \to \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ be defined by $\Phi(r) = 0$ and $\Phi(f) = 1$, and use this to show that $r^nf$ is not conjugate to $r^m$ for any integers $n$ and $m$.
  7. Let $\psi\colon D_6 \to \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ be defined by $\psi(r) = 1$ and $\psi(f) = 0$, and use this to prove that none of the elements in $\{r, rf, r^3, r^3f, r^5, r^5f\}$ are conjugate to any of the elements in $\{e, f, r^2, r^2f, r^4, r^4f\}$.
  8. Let $\varphi\colon D_6 \to S_3$ be the homomorphism defined by $\varphi(f) = (1\ 2)$ and $\varphi(r) = (1\ 2\ 3)$. Note that $S_3$ is not an abelian group, but use this homomorphism to show that $r^3 \in D_6$ is in its own equivalence class.
  9. Partition the dihedral group of the hexagon \[ D_6 = \langle r, f \mid r^6 = f^2 = (rf)^2 = e \rangle \] into conjugacy classes. You don't have to prove every piece of this, but you do have to make a note of any claims that you made that you didn't prove.
