Choice Exercise 7 [3+5+5] (Digital modeling/Maker Studio)

Here is the Mathematica code that I used to solve the system of ODEs to draw curves according to the Frenet-Serret equations: 

curvature[s_]:=s;
torsion[s_]:=s/10;(* When \[Tau]=0, you have to use the hack that \[Tau] = {0,0,0}; *)
tMax=11;
deqSol=NDSolve[
  {
    gamma'[t]==Tv[t],
    Tv'[t]==curvature[t]*Nv[t],
    Nv'[t]==-curvature[t]*Tv[t] + torsion[t]*Bv[t],
    Bv'[t]==-torsion[t]*Nv[t],
    Tv[0]==Normalize[{0,1,1}],
    Nv[0]==Normalize[{1,0,0}],
    Bv[0]==Normalize[{0,-1,1}],
    gamma[0]=={0,0,0}
  },{gamma,Tv,Nv,Bv},{t,0,tMax},AccuracyGoal->10
][[1]];
gammaf=gamma/.deqSol;
Tf=Tv/.deqSol;
Nf=Nv/.deqSol;
Bf=Bv/.deqSol;
Manipulate[
  Grid[{{
    Show[
      ParametricPlot3D[gammaf[t],{t,0,tMax},BoxRatios->Automatic],
      Graphics3D[{PointSize[0.03],Red,Point[gammaf[s]]}],
      Graphics3D[{
        Black,Opacity[0.3],
        InfinitePlane[{gammaf[s],gammaf[s]+Tf[s],gammaf[s]+Nf[s]}]
      }],
      Graphics3D[{
        Red,Arrow@Tube[{gammaf[s],gammaf[s]+Tf[s]}],
        Green,Arrow@Tube[{gammaf[s],gammaf[s]+Nf[s]}],
        Blue,Arrow@Tube[{gammaf[s],gammaf[s]+Bf[s]}]
      }],
      PlotRange->All,ImageSize->Medium
    ],
    Show[
      Plot[{curvature[t],torsion[t]},{t,0,tMax}],
      Graphics[{
        PointSize[0.02],
        Blue,Point[{s,curvature[s]}],
        Orange,Point[{s,torsion[s]}]
      }],ImageSize->Medium
    ]
  }}],
  {s,0,tMax}
]

Choice Exercise 9 [10] (Challenge)

Nash's embedding theorems says that (subject to certain conditions) given \(\mathbb E\), \(\mathbb F\), and \(\mathbb G\), there is always a surface embedded in $\mathbb{R}^3$ whose dot products agree with these. (Like most things in this class, these tend not to have explicit formulas.)

Take the functions \(\mathbb E\), \(\mathbb F\), and \(\mathbb G\) given in class, and see if you can figure out how to get a surface in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) that agrees with them.

If you can, write down what it is, how you found it, and include a plot. If you try for \(10n\) minutes and don't succeed, write down roughly \(n\) sentences describing what you tried, why you don't think your approach worked, and what you'd try next.
